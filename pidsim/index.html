The Quadcopter PID Explorer

This simulator models an acro drone PID controller, for the roll axis only. The drone's roll rate can be controlled with the top left slider, like you would use a RC transmitter. Like a transmitter stick, it will snap back to the center when you release your "grip."

Changing the P, I, or D gain values immediately changes the PID controller.

The model assumes a slight systemic imbalance that will cause the drone to naturally drift.

You can try different drone mass parameters with the upper right slider - for example to see how a lighter drone might behave versus a heavier one.

Some suggested experiments to get you started:

    • Try zeroing out the I and D gains then making quick, halting left and right rolls.
      With I and D zeroed out, try very low and very high P values.
      Try adding in a little D, then try halting rolls again to see what's changed.
      Try adding in a little I gain, and watching how the natural drift changes.
      Try adding larger and larger I values, to see when the model becomes unstable again.
